ABOUT ME

As a GIS Project Manager at Compétences Conseil, I managed various GIS projects across Morocco. My role involved processing, analyzing, and visualizing geospatial data, updating and maintaining GIS databases, conducting field missions for data collection, and creating detailed maps for project analysis and decision-making. I have also gained experience in collaboration within multidisciplinary teams.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Geology and a Master's degree in Applied Geomatics for Geosciences and the Environment. My academic journey included a Bachelor's thesis focused on the study of the Tasiast area's structure using geophysical data and a Master's thesis on the impact of watermelon expansion on groundwater resources in the Feija Basin, employing geostatistical modeling and remote sensing techniques. These experiences have provided me with a strong foundation in geology, GIS, and remote sensing to effectively analyze geological structures, assess environmental impacts, and manage geospatial data for informed decision-making in various contexts.

I hope to continue my career in remote sensing, GIS, and Earth science. I am also passionate about conducting research related to land cover change, ecosystems, water resource evolution, and the threats we face by climate change. My goal is to increase my skills to better understand our environment.
